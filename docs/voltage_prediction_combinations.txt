# Voltage Prediction – Material Combination Logic

1. Predict the cell voltage:
   V_cell = V_cathode - V_anode

2. Common combinations:
   - NMC + Graphite → 3.6–3.7 V nominal
   - NMC + Hard Carbon → +0.05V more
   - LNMO + Graphite → 4.7–4.8 V
   - LNMO + Hard Carbon → up to 4.9 V
   - LCO + Graphite → 3.9–4.2 V
   - LFP + Graphite → 3.2–3.3 V
   - LFP + LTO → 1.9–2.2 V (low voltage)

3. To reach 5V:
   - You need cathode ≥ 4.8V
   - Options:
       • LNMO (Spinel)  
       • Li-rich Mn-based cathodes (LRLO)  
       • High-voltage NMC (doped)

4. To reach 6V:
   - Not possible with standard anodes (graphite)
   - Requires:
       • Li-metal anode
       • Solid electrolyte (LLZO)

5. Voltage Boosting Strategy:
   - Use hard carbon instead of graphite → raises voltage 0.05–0.1V
   - Reduce anode overhang → small voltage increase
