BATTERY MEGA KNOWLEDGE DOCUMENT

----------------------------------------------------
1. BATTERY BASICS
----------------------------------------------------
A battery is an electrochemical device that converts chemical energy into electrical energy.  
Major components:
- Cathode (Positive)
- Anode (Negative)
- Electrolyte (Ion conductor)
- Separator (Ion-permeable insulating layer)
- Current Collectors (Al for cathode, Cu for anode)

Lithium ions move:
- Discharge: Anode → Cathode
- Charge: Cathode → Anode

----------------------------------------------------
2. CATHODE MATERIALS
----------------------------------------------------
LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate)
- High safety, long cycle life, low cost, medium energy density
- Voltage: 3.2–3.3V

NMC (Nickel-Manganese-Cobalt)
- High energy density, used in EVs
- Types: NMC111, NMC532, NMC622, NMC811
- Voltage: 3.6–3.7V

LCO (Lithium Cobalt Oxide)
- High energy density, used in phones
- Voltage: 3.7V

NCA (Nickel-Cobalt-Aluminum)
- High energy & long cycle life, used by Tesla
- Voltage: 3.6–3.7V

----------------------------------------------------
3. ANODE MATERIALS
----------------------------------------------------
Graphite  
- Most common, stable, low cost  
- Limits fast charging due to SEI formation

Silicon-Graphite  
- Higher capacity  
- Silicon expands during cycling → swelling

Hard Carbon  
- For sodium-ion batteries  
- Performs well at low temperatures  

LTO (Lithium Titanate)  
- Very safe, 10k+ cycles, fast charging  
- Low energy density  

----------------------------------------------------
4. ELECTROLYTES
----------------------------------------------------
Typical electrolyte:
- LiPF6 salt in EC/EMC/DEC solvents.

Common additives:
- FEC: Stabilizes SEI
- VC: Controls gas formation
- DTD: Improves cycle life

----------------------------------------------------
5. SEI LAYER (Solid Electrolyte Interphase)
----------------------------------------------------
Forms on anode during initial charging.  
Functions:
- Prevents continuous electrolyte decomposition  
- Enables lithium-ion movement  
Problems:
- Too thick → high resistance  
- Too thin → unstable  

----------------------------------------------------
6. CELL FORMATS
----------------------------------------------------
1. Cylindrical (18650, 21700)
- Strong shell, good thermal performance

2. Prismatic
- High energy density, aluminum casing

3. Pouch
- Lightweight, needs compression

----------------------------------------------------
7. BATTERY MANUFACTURING PROCESS
----------------------------------------------------
1. Mixing (active materials + binders)  
2. Coating on Al/Cu foil  
3. Drying  
4. Calendering (density control)  
5. Slitting  
6. Stacking/Winding  
7. Electrolyte filling  
8. Formation (SEI creation)  
9. Aging  
10. Testing  

----------------------------------------------------
8. SAFETY & FAILURE MODES
----------------------------------------------------
Failures:
- Internal short circuit
- Thermal runaway
- Lithium plating
- Gas generation
- Separator shrinkage

Safety mechanisms:
- BMS
- CID, PTC
- Additives (FEC/VC)

----------------------------------------------------
9. BATTERY DEGRADATION
----------------------------------------------------
- SEI growth
- Lithium plating
- Particle cracking
- Electrolyte decomposition
- Increased internal resistance  

----------------------------------------------------
10. LFP VS NMC
----------------------------------------------------
LFP:
- Safer, cheaper, long life  
- Medium energy density  

NMC:
- Higher energy  
- More expensive  
- Moderate safety  

----------------------------------------------------
11. BATTERY PACK COMPONENTS
----------------------------------------------------
- Cells (series/parallel)  
- BMS  
- Cooling system  
- Contactor  
- Fuse  
- Busbars  
- Enclosure  

----------------------------------------------------
12. BASIC BATTERY CALCULATIONS
----------------------------------------------------
Energy = Voltage × Capacity  
Pack Voltage = Cell voltage × Series count  
Pack Ah = Ah × Parallel count  
Heat loss = I² × R  

----------------------------------------------------
13. BATTERY COMBINATIONS
----------------------------------------------------
- LFP + LTO → ultra-safe, long life  
- Graphite + Silicon composite → high energy  
- LMFP (Mn-doped LFP) → higher voltage  
- Hard carbon + sodium cathode → sodium-ion systems
