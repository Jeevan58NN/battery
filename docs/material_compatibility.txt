MATERIAL COMPATIBILITY RULES

1. Anode–Electrolyte Compatibility:
- Graphite requires SEI-forming additives (VC, FEC)
- Silicon–graphite needs high FEC content (5–10%)
- LTO requires no SEI → high stability

2. Cathode–Electrolyte Compatibility:
- LFP works with standard electrolyte
- NMC/NCA require electrolyte with additives (VC, PS)
- High-voltage LMO (4.7V) needs fluorinated electrolyte

3. Temperature Rules:
Low temperature (<10°C)
- Silicon performs poorly → lithium plating risk
- Recommend graphite or LTO anode

High temperature (>45°C)
- Avoid high-Ni cathodes (NMC811 or NCA)
- Prefer LFP or LMO

4. If user asks:
"I am using ANODE X + ELECTROLYTE Y at TEMPERATURE Z. What cathode?"
Bot must check:
- Stability window
- Anode potential
- Electrolyte compatibility
- Required voltage

5. Examples:
Graphite anode + standard electrolyte + room temp → LFP or NMC
Graphite anode + HV electrolyte → 4.7V LMO
Silicon composite + strong SEI-forming electrolyte → NMC 811 or NCA
LTO anode → must use high-voltage cathode to recover voltage
